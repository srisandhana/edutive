Chapter 1 - Revision of Rational Numbers.doc
Overview

The chapter covers the important theorems related to real numbers such as the fundamental theorem of arithmetic, Euclid's division lemma, and the uniqueness of prime factorization. The chapter also covers the concept of decimal representation of rational numbers and the relationship between decimals and fractions.

Objective

1. Introduce students to the concept of real numbers, rational and irrational numbers, and their properties.

2. Familiarize students with the important theorems related to real numbers such as the fundamental theorem of arithmetic, Euclid's division lemma, and the uniqueness of prime factorization.

3. Help students understand the relationship between decimals and fractions and be able to represent rational numbers in decimal form.

4. Develop problem-solving skills in students by providing exercises and examples that require the application of concepts related to real numbers.

5. Prepare students for higher-level mathematics by providing a foundation of knowledge and skills related to real numbers that will be necessary in more advanced math courses.
